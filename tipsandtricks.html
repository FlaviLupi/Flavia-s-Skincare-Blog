Even if you have oily skin, moisturizing is crucial. Look for oil-free, non-comedogenic moisturizers for oily skin or richer creams for dry skin. Consider using a moisturizer with hyaluronic acid, which is great for keeping the skin hydrated and glowing.

Exfoliation helps remove dead skin cells and reveal fresh, glowing skin. Use a gentle exfoliator (physical or chemical) once or twice a week. Be cautious not to over-exfoliate, as it can irritate your skin.

Always wear sunscreen, even on cloudy days or indoors near windows! UV damage can cause premature aging, dark spots, and dull skin. Opt for a broad-spectrum SPF of at least 30.

Ingredients like vitamin C, niacinamide, and green tea can brighten your complexion, even out skin tone, and protect your skin from damage. Consider using a vitamin C serum in the morning for extra glow.

High stress levels can trigger breakouts and dullness. Practice stress management techniques like meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises. Regular exercise also helps reduce stress and increases blood circulation, which benefits your skin.

Sleep is when your body (including your skin) repairs itself. Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night for that refreshed, healthy glow. Lack of sleep can lead to dull, tired skin and even acne breakouts.
